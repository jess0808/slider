THE SCOOP

Learn how ice cream is made and other fun facts about the world's favorite frozen treat .

1700s

Ice cream is introduced in America but is considered a delicacy for the upper class.

1776

The first ice cream parlor in America opens in New York.

1840s

The ice cream churn is invented.

1880s

The ice cream sundae is born.

1904

The first waffle cone makes its appearance at the World's Fair in St. Louis.

1929

Rocky Road ice cream becomes the first widely available flavor.

1984

National ice cream month is declared in July.

The three basic ingredients used in the making of ice cream are milk, eggs, and sugar.
Click on the items below to learn about their importance.

MILK

This is the most crucial ingredient. Milk affects smoothness and texture of ice cream.

EGG YOLKS

Eggs aren't necessary but they create a rich flavor. They also prolong shelf life of ice cream and make it soft
and creamy!

SUGAR

Sugar limits the amount of ice formed during the freezing process to make sure ice cream is soft enough to scoop
and eat.

Blending

The basic ingredients are blended in a mixing tank. This is the most important step of the process!

Pasteurization & homogenization

The mixture is heated to kill bacteria and to make it smooth and creamy.

Freezing

Air cells are created during cooling and freezing to prevent mixture from turning into a solid frozen mass.

Flavorings & toppings

Ingredients are dropped into the mixture after it leaves the freezer.

Hardening

Mixture is frozen again at subzero temperatures.
